Using Social Support to Manage Stress

Social support means getting help, encouragement, or understanding from other people such as friends, family members, classmates, mentors, or counselors. Reaching out to others can make stress feel less heavy and remind students that they do not have to handle challenges alone.

What This Technique Is

Social support is the process of turning to trusted people for emotional comfort, advice, encouragement, or practical help. For college students, this can include talking with friends, checking in with family, meeting with classmates, or asking a campus resource for guidance.

When You Would Use It

  • Feeling overwhelmed
  • Experiencing emotional stress
  • Needing advice or guidance
  • Feeling isolated

How and Why It Works

  • Reduces feelings of isolation
  • Helps process emotions
  • Provides different perspectives
  • Can offer solutions or support

How to Do It

  • Talk to a friend
  • Reach out to family
  • Join a study group
  • Ask for help when needed

Try It Now

Text or talk to one person today about how you’re feeling.
