Deep Breathing for Stress Management

Deep breathing is a simple relaxation technique that helps calm the body, slow racing thoughts, and reduce the physical feelings of stress. It can be used almost anywhere and only takes a few moments to practice.

What This Technique Is

Deep breathing is a purposeful way of slowing and controlling your breath so your body can shift away from a stress response. When practiced regularly, it can help students feel more grounded, focused, and relaxed.

When You Would Use It

  • Before exams
  • During anxiety
  • When overwhelmed

How and Why It Works

  • Slows heart rate
  • Calms the nervous system
  • Reduces stress and anxiety

How to Do It

  1. Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  2. Hold your breath gently for 4 seconds.
  3. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 4 to 6 seconds.
  4. Repeat the cycle several times until your body begins to feel calmer.

Try It Now

Take 3 slow breaths now and notice how your body feels after each exhale.
